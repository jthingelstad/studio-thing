Trying [Sous Vides brisket](https://www.seriouseats.com/recipes/2016/08/sous-vide-barbecue-smoked-bbq-brisket-texas-recipe.html) for the first time. Trimmed down the flat. Simple salt and pepper. Texas style. Cut in half to vacuum seal for the bath. In the 155 °F water bath for 36 hours. Will finish on [Big Green Egg](https://biggreenegg.com) for 3-4 hours for smoke and bark on Saturday.

<img src="https://www.thingelstad.com/uploads/2020/283845fbb5.jpg" width="600" height="449" alt="Seasoned beef brisket in a vacuum seal bag submerged in a clear plastic water bath container with an immersion circulator attached" />

24 hours into the Sous Vides and all proceeding well!

<img src="https://www.thingelstad.com/uploads/2020/d8b9eb9d3c.jpg" alt="Seasoned beef brisket vacuum-sealed in a FoodSaver bag submerged in a clear polycarbonate sous vide water bath container with an immersion circulator attached, sitting on a wire cooling rack on a">

37 hours of Sous Vides complete. Pulled the brisket out and leaving it in bags to cool in the fridge. Will smoke for 3-4 hours to finish this afternoon. 🤤

<img src="https://www.thingelstad.com/uploads/2020/647df1d2e8.jpg" alt="Two vacuum-sealed FoodSaver bags of raw brisket with dark marinade, resting in a glass baking dish and a metal sheet pan on a white countertop.">

After a few hours cooking in the refrigerator I pulled the brisket out of the bags. I put the juice aside to use for something else. Second application of salt and pepper rub, and onto the Big Green Egg to smoke for three or so hours.
