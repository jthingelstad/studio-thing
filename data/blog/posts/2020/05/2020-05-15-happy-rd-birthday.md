On this day, 23 years ago, we launched [BigCharts.com](https://bigcharts.marketwatch.com/). The time before and after that was fun, stressful, and filled with unknowns. It was a great ride. One of the defining experiences of my life. I love that the site is still around, serving charts for all that want! I’m sure code I wrote is still running on it. 😬

I bet that it is one of the very few sites that can show a graph of the Dow Jones Industrial Average back to 1970. I know because I was the one that backloaded that data into the database!

<img src="https://www.thingelstad.com/uploads/2020/749d60b164.jpg" width="600" height="364" alt="DJIA yearly candlestick chart from 1970 to 2020 on a log scale showing long-term growth from under 1000 to near 29000 with a momentum indicator below." />

I love that the size of chart bigger than Large is still called **Big**! I remember the laughter when we came up with that obvious label. 

It still is one of the few sites that will give you a [historical quote](https://bigcharts.marketwatch.com/historical/default.asp?symb=Msft&closeDate=5%2F16%2F12&x=0&y=0), with the price info for a given day in the past, backing out splits. 

I remember the [up2.aspx](https://bigcharts.marketwatch.com/up2.aspx) page that we used for the load balancers to make sure servers were healthy. 

Here is a team photo from June 9, 1999. It was a tech team dinner at Buca. What a great team!

<img src="https://www.thingelstad.com/uploads/2020/57c62c7544.jpg" width="600" height="400" alt="Large group of roughly 20 people seated around a long table with red-checked tablecloths at Buca di Beppo restaurant, smiling at the camera." />

I hope the site enjoys its 20s and is around for decades to come!
