Working through my first pool “issue”. The Saltwater Chlorine Generator was set too low (6%) while we were gone and the chlorine level dropped to near nil. Water was cloudy, but no color. I got [HTH® Super Shock! Treatment](https://www.hthpools.com/pool/pool-products/shock/super-shock-treatment-1lb) and added 6.9 lbs to the pool. The water turned green right after adding the granules. Now waiting and hoping it all works out okay. 🤞💧

<img src="https://www.thingelstad.com/uploads/2020/34eba2f9d6.jpg" width="600" height="450" alt="Rectangular backyard swimming pool with green-tinted cloudy water, a gray plastic slide on the far side, concrete deck surround, shed and trees in background." />

Added another 2.3 lbs of Super Shock! Treatment later in the evening. Can smell chlorine now, before there was no pool chlorine smell. Did a water test and the Total Chlorine was around 10 ppm, but Free Chlorine is still near 0.

<img src="https://www.thingelstad.com/uploads/2020/688c36d3d8.jpg" width="600" height="450" alt="Backyard swimming pool with green algae-tinted water, blue tile border, a diving board on the right, and a blue hose visible at the far end." />

Next morning the water is much less green, but still very green. The cloudy seems to be gone. Water test shows total chlorine around 5 ppm but free chlorine at 0. I thought I didn’t add enough chlorine so added 4.16  lbs [HTH Shock Treatment](https://www.hthpools.com/pool/pool-products/shock/shock-treatment-5lbs). We had to take off so I left the Chlorine Generator in Boost Mode, it will go back to 20% after the boost. I also added about 2/3 gallon of muriatic acid since the pH level was very high.
