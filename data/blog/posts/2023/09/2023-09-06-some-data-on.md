Some data on this summers heat. 

> The past three months have been the hottest ever measured, according to the **[Copernicus Climate Change Service](https://climate.copernicus.eu)**, an [EU-funded programme](https://www.economist.com/leaders/2023/07/27/the-worlds-poor-need-to-know-about-weather-disasters-ahead-of-time). Last month was the warmest August on record — and the second hottest month ever, after July 2023 — with temperatures an estimated 1.5°C above preindustrial averages. In August sea-surface temperatures also hit 20.98°C, their highest monthly average. — Economist Espresso, Sep 6 2023
