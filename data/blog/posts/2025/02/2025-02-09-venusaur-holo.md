I had never heard of a [Gacha](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gacha_game) before but I was intrigued when [Collector Crypt](https://collectorcrypt.com) sent an announcement about the [Elite Pokémon Gacha Machine](https://gacha.collectorcrypt.com). I forwarded it to Tyler to investigate. 

<img src="https://www.thingelstad.com/uploads/2025/gacha-machine.png" alt="Collector Crypt Gacha Machine website showing six graded Pokemon cards available as prizes, with an Elite Pokemon Gacha Pack priced at 50 tokens on the right.">

The gist is you pay a fixed amount and get a random card with a minimum value. Collector Crypt does this all on the Solana blockchain. We decided to give it a whirl and pulled a Venusaur Holo (1996) with a near-mint 8 PSA rating. 

<img src="https://www.thingelstad.com/uploads/2025/fbd99f708b.jpg" alt="A PSA-graded Japanese Venusaur holographic Pokémon card with a rating of NM-MT 8 from 1996.">

Tyler digs Collector Crypt because they have good Pokémon cards. I dig it for that and that it is a great use of NFT's and smart contracts. The [transaction](https://explorer.solana.com/tx/2wFoFe2dVUbHD5nd7LQiS5Qbb7J989WM6E4Rk5Mg3h8fvftKeoyEcGmtUFaJHcBbX4yXUmdLoJHpYtWJVnhUSm9r) for this Gacha machine is a complicated one with seventeen different steps. 

We will likely "burn" the NFT and have Collector Crypt ship us the nearly 30 year old card.
