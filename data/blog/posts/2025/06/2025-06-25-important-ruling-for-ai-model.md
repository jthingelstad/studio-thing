Important ruling for AI model training, copyright, and fair use. This aligns with how I would think about it. 

> A federal judge in America ruled it was "fair use" for **Anthropic**, an [AI lab](https://www.economist.com/science-and-technology/2025/06/18/how-to-find-the-smartest-ai), to train its chatbot on books [without authors' permission](https://www.economist.com/the-world-ahead/2024/11/20/the-court-cases-that-could-shape-how-ai-develops). Its storage of over 7m pirated books, however, was not. Last year three authors sued Anthropic for allegedly copying and storing pirated material without compensation; the judge said Anthropic will face a separate trial to determine the damages it owes. — Economist, World in Brief, June 25, 2025
