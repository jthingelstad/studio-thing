One of the ways that a [blog can be a gift](https://www.thingelstad.com/2025/04/03/blogging-as-a-gift.html) is to give others the option to go on journeys with you to [locations far away](https://www.thingelstad.com/2025/04/06/telling-stories-with-maps.html). In 2023 our family took a [trip to Switzerland & Italy](https://www.thingelstad.com/collections/switzerland-italy-2023/), and we spent four wonderful days in Florence (day [14](https://www.thingelstad.com/2023/07/27/switzerland-italy-log.html), [15](https://www.thingelstad.com/2023/07/28/switzerland-italy-log.html), [16](https://www.thingelstad.com/2023/07/29/switzerland-italy-log.html), and [17](https://www.thingelstad.com/2023/07/30/switzerland-italy-log.html)). It was a great (if not hot!) time. 

Since we had been there just two years ago it piqued my interest when I saw fellow blogger [Barry Hess](https://bjhess.com/) was going there with his family to visit their daughter that is there for school. And what a gift Barry gave to himself and us with these wonderful daily recaps of their trip. It was fun to remember our own trip, and to see what they explored over a longer stay in Florence.

Here is each of his posts by day with great overviews and photos.

- [Florence: Arrivo](https://bjhess.com/posts/florence-arrivo) (Mar 27)
- [Florence: Rimpinzati di Cibo](https://bjhess.com/posts/florence-rimpinzati-di-cibo) (Mar 28)
- [Florence: Arti e Musica](https://bjhess.com/posts/florence-arti-e-musica) (Mar 29)
- [Florence: Arti II e Calcio](https://bjhess.com/posts/florence-arti-ii-e-calcio) (Mar 30)
- [Florence: Giornata Libera](https://bjhess.com/posts/florence-giornata-libera) (Mar 31)
