After using GPT-5 to [create category introductions](https://www.thingelstad.com/2025/08/14/category-introductions-via-llm.html) for my website I got to thinking about the categories themselves. 

With over **9,800 blog posts** spanning **20+ years** it is daunting to figure out an approach to categories. It is especially daunting to approach them in a way that would make sense to a visitor to my website. As a test, I gave this challenge to GPT-5 using Agent mode. It took _31 minutes_ to do the research. I have no idea how many web pages it hit on my site (I wasn't watching!). It gave me a whole report and the results seem really solid.

<div class="chat">
<div class="author right">Me</div>
<div class="message right">

I would like you to do an extensive analysis of my blog archive at this URL:

[www.thingelstad.com/archive/](https://www.thingelstad.com/archive/)

I would like you to recommend a new set of categories I could use for my blog posts that may be easier and more useful to visitors. You can feel free to ignore existing categories or use as input into the process. I'm fine if there are a lot of categories, I also wouldn't want hundreds. It feels like 30-50 categories may be fine, but I'm open to a different count. There are over 20 years of blog posts to categorize. I'm fine if categories only exist for a period of time, they don't all have to have content throughout the life of my blog.

Each one of these is also provided a description and examples posts that would fit into it. This seems like a great place to use AI.

Going through my blog and assigning posts to these categories could be done with a script and some vibe coding. Perhaps this will be a winter project for me.

All of this has me thinking that it would be nice for micro.blog to do this for me. I wouldn't mind having micro.blog use AI to **recommend categories** for me. But then separately, now that we have categories with introduction text take each of my blog posts and ask the LLM to categories it into the existing structure. All the data is there. 🤔
