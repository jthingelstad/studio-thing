With many thanks to [Amit Gawande](https://amitgawande.com/)'s fun [Post Stats Plugin](https://amitgawande.com/blog/2024/year-recap-mb) and a recent [discussion](https://micro.blog/jthingelstad/52635531) to add some more information to it — I’m sharing some perspective on my blogging from 2024.

In 2024 I published **701 posts** with **51,905 words**. Of those, **529 had photos** and **157 of them had titles**. 

June had about triple the normal blogging activity which was from our [trip to Ireland](https://www.thingelstad.com/collections/ireland-2024/). Over the last few years I've done daily travel logs on those trips and usually write a blog post for each activity. It can be a lot, but I value what it creates and know that in future years I will revisit those posts with many fond memories. 

<img src="https://www.thingelstad.com/uploads/2025/posts-published-2024.png" width="600" height="239" alt="Auto-generated description: A line graph shows the number of posts published each month, with a peak in June and a smaller rise in December.">

Zooming out I now have a total of **9,111 posts** containing **574,102 words**! My blog posts have come along many migrations:

.Text [→](https://www.thingelstad.com/2006/11/25/migrating-text-to.html) WordPress [→](https://www.thingelstad.com/2016/04/20/moved-my-blog.html) SquareSpace [→](https://www.thingelstad.com/2014/03/16/i-flopped-httpstcozmirtevd.html) Pelican [→](https://www.thingelstad.com/2017/10/29/managing-years-of.html) Jekyll [→](https://www.thingelstad.com/2020/05/02/importing-jekyll-site.html) micro.blog

On top of that I imported my Twitter archive and curated and improved that. Since April 2017 I've been publishing on micro.blog and you can see that it has enabled me to post even more. To me, this is one of the best ways to evaluate a blogging platform — does it enable you to "just write" on the web.
