Our [month of eating vegan](https://www.thingelstad.com/2011/09/30/day-vegan-challenge.html) has a lot of new challenges. One of the biggest is what to do with my cherished Big Green Egg's. I liked that ratatouille is naturally vegan, and it seemed that cooking it on the [Big Green Egg](http://biggreenegg.com/) would be really good. I followed the [Ratatouille's Ratatouille recipe on Smitten Kitchen](http://smittenkitchen.com/2007/07/rat-a-too-ee-for-you-ee/) and instead of putting it in the oven I put it on the large Big Green Egg set up for indirect cooking.

<img src="https://www.thingelstad.com/uploads/2020/d998f39598.jpg" alt="Sliced zucchini, eggplant, and bell pepper arranged in overlapping rows in a red ceramic baking dish on a charcoal grill.">

It came out really good with a nice, light smoke flavor infused as well. I served it on a bed of couscous.

<img src="https://www.thingelstad.com/uploads/2020/9694168e2a.jpg" alt="Sliced zucchini, eggplant, and tomato ratatouille topped with tomato sauce served over a bed of couscous on a white plate.">

Vegan? Check. Grill? Check. Delicious? Check.

When it comes to grills and eating vegan there isn't much overlap. However, the Big Green Egg is unique since it can do anything an oven can do, but better.
