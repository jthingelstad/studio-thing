A few days ago I watched this amazing video that was created on the
brand new [Canon 5D Mark
II](http://www.usa.canon.com/consumer/controller?act=ModelInfoAct&fcategoryid=139&modelid=17662).
I was going to put a link to it on my site, but when I went to found it
I was greeted with this screen.

<img src="https://www.thingelstad.com/uploads/2020/215aaebd05.png" style="max-width: 499px; " />

What? [Canon USA](http://www.usa.canon.com/) has "EXCEEDED \[THEIR\]
DOWNLOAD CAPACITY" (the all-caps is courtesy of them). Does anyone at
Canon realize how ridiculous it makes them look to have this message on
their site? I'm wondering if maybe Canon just hosts at Dreamhost or
something.

Canon, if you are going to tell people about how serious you are about
amazing imaging and beautiful video, the first place to start is by
being able to serve that content to the web. And please never use [ALL
CAPS](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/All_caps).

Apparently the video will be back up "shortly" and I'll post a link to
it then.
