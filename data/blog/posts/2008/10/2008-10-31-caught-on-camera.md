When I worked downtown I was really lucky to have this very cool independent coffee shop just a block away from the office. I made a daily trip, even in the freezing cold of January, to get a coffee from [One on One](http://www.oneononebike.com/). Now that I'm not downtown all the time I don't get there as often, but it seems I did make it into the background on a recent photo shoot!

<img src="https://www.thingelstad.com/uploads/2020/33ada0627f.png" alt="Two people stand inside a bike shop surrounded by bicycles and warm interior lighting." width="520" height="1401" style="max-width: 520px !important;" />

I was there a couple of weeks ago and I remember them moving some bikes around and it seemed a bit goofy. I saw a photographer and just figured they were doing some promotional shots for the website or something. Turns out it was actually for [an article in the Star Tribune](http://www.startribune.com/business/33478314.html) and I snuck into the background. See the top-left corner.

<img src="https://www.thingelstad.com/uploads/2020/b46273e1df.jpg" alt="Two people sit at a table inside a coffee shop with a bicycle propped nearby; a third person is visible in the background at the counter." width="175" height="260" style="max-width: 175px !important;" />

If you are in the area and like coffee, bikes or both stop by. I highly [recommend the cold press coffee](https://www.thingelstad.com/2008/08/14/reasons-to-love.html), this is where I was turned on to the wonders of that stuff.

By the way, the [Robin Williams](http://www.robinwilliams.com/) visit mentioned in that article was captured in person at the end of [my Farewell video](https://www.thingelstad.com/2008/05/10/farewell-video.html) by my buddies [Jim](http://www.jimbernard.net/) and [Kent](http://www.thetangens.net/).
