Today Tammy put together this years custom [Christmas Puzzle](https://www.thingelstad.com/family/christmas-puzzles/) that we made for her and it came out really well. It was definitely the hardest puzzle we have created yet. We titled it "Land of Things".

<img src="https://www.thingelstad.com/uploads/2021/9da8ce844c.jpg" width="600" height="450" alt="Illustrated map titled Land of Things showing colorful island towns connected by waterways with a sea dragon, octopus, whale, and tiny figures" />

And here it is put together in 18x24" 500 piece jigsaw puzzle.

<img src="https://www.thingelstad.com/uploads/2021/5a175570e0.jpg" width="600" height="450" alt="Completed 500-piece jigsaw puzzle titled Land of Things showing a colorful illustrated map of fantasy islands with small buildings, on a wooden table." />

We took a unique approach this year. Tyler and I have been having fun with [Townscaper](https://www.townscapergame.com) so I asked Tyler to create the overall base of the puzzle using Townscaper. He used the iPad and spent a while creating various islands and buildings. We thematically tied each location to houses and neighborhoods we have lived in. We also included our cabin as an island that you had to get to by boat.

After we had the scene in Townscaper I loaded the city on the desktop version of Townscaper in Steam because it could render it larger and export a higher quality image. Using a screenshot on the iPad wasn't high enough resolution. 

I took that image and then we loaded it into Procreate and Mazie went to work drawing on top of it. We added grass, various scenes, decorated some things, a big sea dragon, etc. The details were really great.
