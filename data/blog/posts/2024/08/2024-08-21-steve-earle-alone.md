Last night Tammy and I saw [Steve Earle](http://www.steveearle.com) at the [Dakota](https://www.dakotacooks.com). Mazie and I saw him [last year at the Dakota](https://www.thingelstad.com/2023/08/10/it-was-great.html).  I also saw Earle's [Copperhead Road 30th Anniversary show in 2018](https://www.thingelstad.com/2018/03/21/steve-earle-doing.html) and saw him at the [Minnesota Zoo in 2017](https://www.thingelstad.com/2017/07/30/steve-earle-now.html). I’m a fan of Steve Earle and love these solo acoustic shows.

He played for nearly two hours and [covered a big part of his catalog](https://www.setlist.fm/setlist/steve-earle/2024/the-dakota-minneapolis-mn-1b554d60.html). I loved his comment about writing "[Tom Ames' Prayer](https://genius.com/Robert-earl-keen-tom-ames-prayer-lyrics)" when he was 20 and "[God is God](https://genius.com/Steve-earle-god-is-god-lyrics)" in his 50s. 

He shared one of his favorite quotes from Townes Van Zandt:

> There are only two kinds of songs; there's the blues, and there's zip-a-dee-doo-dah.

He called out to his "teachers" with songs and stories: [Townes Van Zandt](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Townes_Van_Zandt), [Jerry Jeff Walker](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jerry_Jeff_Walker), and [Guy Clark](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Guy_Clark).

It also happened that the show was on the 4 year anniversary of his son [Justin Townes Earle](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Justin_Townes_Earle) death from fentanyl. This was the second time I'd heard him talk about the loss of his son. So very sad.

### Shannon McNally

[Shannon McNally](https://www.shannonmcnally.com) opened the show with a solid set that I enjoyed a lot as well. She shared great stories of the road and her songs were inspiring.
