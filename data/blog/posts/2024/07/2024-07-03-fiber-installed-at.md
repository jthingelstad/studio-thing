I [met with Bevcomm on July 1 2023](https://www.thingelstad.com/2023/07/01/bevcomm-is-the.html) to give them the go ahead to install fiber at our cabin. They thought it would take about a year and today — one year and two days later — it was installed and activated! [Bevcomm](https://www.bevcomm.net/site/) sent two technicians out and everything went very smooth.

I had made several upgrades to the network infrastructure in preparation. I don't have ethernet here and was using [ethernet-over-power adapters](https://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B01MTNKNPZ/) before with [Eero](https://eero.com) infrastructure. 

I knew I wanted to switch everything to [UniFi](https://www.ui.com). I use UniFi at home and am very pleased with it. I decided to use the existing coax wiring for the backbone using [MoCa adapters](https://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B08MQG6T61/). Each of the four MoCa adapter then has a little 4-port switch. I've also got 2 [WiFi 6 LR access points](https://techspecs.ui.com/unifi/wifi/u6-lr?s=us) and a UniFi [U6 Mesh](https://techspecs.ui.com/unifi/wifi/u6-mesh) access point that is installed outside. I've now got direct line-of-site for my external network devices. External WiFi signal is way better!

Today the last part clicked in with the fiber getting turned on. I provisioned 500 Mbps service. I didn’t think full gigabit made sense. Speeds look great and response times are very snappy.
