- AWS ECS for Kubernetes announcement makes total sense. Expected and good to see them adopting this.
- The new AWS [EC2 Bare Metal Instance](https://aws.amazon.com/blogs/aws/new-amazon-ec2-bare-metal-instances-with-direct-access-to-hardware/) seems like an RFP checkbox requirement. Or very rare edge case need. 
- Multi-master multi-region Aurora sounds great.
- [AWS Neptune](https://aws.amazon.com/blogs/aws/amazon-neptune-a-fully-managed-graph-database-service/) has some promise to make graph databases more reliable and usable for applications.
- The _house band_ idea during the AWS re:invent keynote seems really odd. Skip… 
- [AWS S3 Select](https://aws.amazon.com/blogs/aws/s3-glacier-select/) sounds very cool.
- [AWS SageMaker](https://aws.amazon.com/blogs/aws/sagemaker/) has promise to bring machine learning to complex problems faster and cheaper.
- [AWS DeepLens](https://aws.amazon.com/blogs/aws/deeplens/) looks like a solution looking for a problem. Cool tech, don't need it at all.
- New [AWS Transcribe](https://aws.amazon.com/blogs/aws/amazon-transcribe-scalable-and-accurate-automatic-speech-recognition/) should enable some nice speech-to-text features in various applications.
- 2h 46m AWS Keynote. Too long.
