Last weekend we finished putting in the horseshoe pits at the cabin and I'm very pleased with the result!

<img src="https://www.thingelstad.com/uploads/2020/a4d3435bc8.jpg" alt="Two freshly built wooden horseshoe pits with throwing platforms and backboards set in a grassy backyard, with evergreen trees behind them.">

We have a lot of yard games at the cabin and I though it would be a lot of fun to add horseshoes to the mix. I honestly didn't think that much about it and put a horseshoe set on my wishlist for Christmas. After getting the set I realized I had bit off more than I realized.

<img src="https://www.thingelstad.com/uploads/2020/c59a2c24e8.jpg" alt="Newly built wooden horseshoe pit with a sand landing area, metal stake, backboard, and two side throwing platforms on a grass lawn.">

I found a great site for [horseshoe plans](http://horseshupit.com/Horseshoe_Pit_Builder.html). My brother-in-law helped me get the wood and cutting done and unfortunately the winter came before I got it all done. We finished digging the pits into the ground this spring after the ground thawed.

<img src="https://www.thingelstad.com/uploads/2020/9e94788aa2.jpg" alt="Two wooden horseshoe pits set into a grassy lawn, each with sand-filled throwing areas, metal stake pins, side benches, and a backboard holding two horseshoes.">

I really like the throwing platforms and the backboard. This is a more complex pit than you absolutely have to have, but it's really nice to play on. Now I just have to work on my game!
